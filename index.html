What to expect

The service

Sunday morning service starts at 10am and runs about 90 minutes. You'll hear contemporary worship music led by Cristie Barone, followed by a message from Pastor Paul grounded in Scripture. Come as you are — there's no dress code and no pressure.

Kids & families

Families are welcome in the main service. If you have questions about children's programming before your first visit, give us a call at 207-657-1500 and we'll get you sorted out ahead of time.

Parking & location

We meet at 14 Lewiston Road in Gray — off Route 202, easy to find from New Gloucester, Windham, or Poland. Parking is available on-site. The building is accessible.

First time?

No pressure to join anything or talk to anyone. Show up, grab a seat, and get a feel for the place. If you want to connect, Pastor Paul will be around after the service — he's easy to spot and glad to meet you.

About us

Crossroads Community Church is an Assemblies of God congregation that has been meeting in Gray, Maine since 2014. We believe the Bible is God's Word — accurate, authoritative, and relevant to everyday life — and that the church's calling is to make disciples, not just fill seats.

Our vision: equip people to live and speak God's hope and healing in our community. That happens Sunday mornings, Wednesday afternoons, and through outreach ministries that serve neighbors in practical, tangible ways throughout the week.

Pastor Paul Barone has led the church since 2019. He's a New Yorker who came to Maine in 2014 and put down roots. Cristie Barone leads music ministry on Sunday mornings.

Our Mission

Jesus gave the church one overriding command: make disciples of all nations (Matthew 28:19–20). Everything Crossroads does flows from that. We exist to bring people into a genuine relationship with God — and then equip them to live that out in Gray, Maine, and beyond.

We do that through Sunday morning teaching grounded in Scripture, mid-week Bible study, and outreach ministries that put the gospel into action with food, clothing, and practical support. The Holy Spirit empowers all of it. We don't do this in our own strength.

Our Values

Value 01

Intentional in the Word

The Bible is our authority — not tradition, opinion, or the mood of the culture. Sunday services, Wednesday studies, and everything we teach is built on Scripture. We pursue God's Word actively: weekly as a congregation, and daily as individuals.

Value 02

Intentional in Worship

Worship is an offering, not a performance. We come to give something, not to be entertained. That means every Sunday is an act of surrender — aligning our lives with God's purposes and declaring His worth together as a congregation.

Value 03

Intentional in Community

Faith without action is hollow. We serve neighbors with food, clothing, and practical support through Bread of Life and Crossroads Connect. Genuine care for people — especially those who are struggling — is how we make the gospel visible in Gray.

What We Believe

Scripture

The Bible is God's inspired, accurate, and authoritative Word — the final standard for faith and everyday life. We read it, teach it, and build our lives on it.

The Trinity

One God in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Each is fully God. God is holy, loving, and personally involved in the lives of people.

Jesus Christ

Jesus is fully God and fully man. He lived without sin, died on the cross as the only payment for sin, rose bodily from the dead, and is the only way to God.

Salvation

Salvation is a gift — received by faith in Jesus Christ alone, not earned by good works. It is available to every person who repents and trusts in Him.

The Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit lives in every believer and empowers the church. We believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit and the continuing work of His gifts in the church today.

Divine Healing

God heals today. We believe in praying for the sick and trust that God — in His sovereignty — still works miraculously in people's bodies and lives.

The Church

The church is not a building — it is the body of people called to represent Jesus in the world, make disciples, and care for one another in community.

Eternity

Jesus is returning. Those who have trusted in Him will spend eternity with God. We hold this hope without apology, and it shapes the urgency of everything we do.

Crossroads Community Church is affiliated with the Assemblies of God and holds to its Statement of Fundamental Truths.

Ministries

Community Outreach

Bread of Life

We provide food, clothing, and practical support to neighbors in need. Led by Steve Dunn, Bread of Life is how we put our faith into action in the Gray community — not a program, but a consistent commitment to serving people where they are.

Connection

Crossroads Connect

The starting point for anyone who wants to get more involved — whether you're new to the church or have been around for years. A practical way to find your place in the congregation.

Wednesdays · 12:30pm

Bible Study

Doreen Dunn facilitates a Wednesday afternoon Bible study at 12:30pm. A smaller group setting for people who want to go deeper into Scripture and conversation outside of Sunday morning.

Men's Ministry

Men of Crossroads

Led by Deacon Bryan Lewis — one of the church's longest-standing members — focused on discipleship, accountability, and teaching Scripture among men. Originally from Alabama, Bryan felt called to Maine and has been committed to this community ever since.

Sundays · 10am

Music Ministry

Cristie Barone leads worship every Sunday morning. Contemporary music directed toward genuine worship — not a concert, not background music. Something in between that invites the congregation to actually participate.

Online

Online Church

Can't make it in person? Services are available through our online church portal and on YouTube. The full service experience, from anywhere in Maine or beyond.
